Resto-Style Sweet and Sour Pork in 30 minutes

That unmistakable aroma of a Chinese restaurant says it all. Before you even see the menu, you know a great meal awaits—and sweet and sour pork is always a must. Golden bites coated in that glossy, tangy sauce are simply irresistible.

No need to order out to enjoy this favorite. This sweet and sour pork comes together in 30 minutes or less, giving you crisp meat and a rich sauce—no sogginess, no waiting.

Ingrdients

  • 500g pork (Liempo, kasim, or pigue), cut into 1 inch cubes
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 pc egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup conrstarch
  • canola oil, for deep frying

For Sweet and Sour sauce

  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 3 gloves garlic, sliced thinly
  • 4 pcs shallots, halved
  • 1 pc small red bell pepper, deseeded and cubed
  • 2 tablespoon banana ketchup
  • 1 (115g) pack pineapple tidbits
  • 1 pc Knorr Chicken Cube
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoon conrstarch, dissolved in 1 cup water

Cooking Method

Step 1

Season pork with salt and pepper. Add egg and conrstarch; mixt to coat.

Step 2

Heat oil in a pan. Fry pork one at a time until golden brown. Set aside

Step 3

Make the sweet and sour sauce: Heat oil in a saucepan over medium flame. Saute garlic and shallots until fragrant.

Step 4

Add bell peppers, banana ketchup, pineapple tidbits, Knorr Chicken Cube, white vinegar, and sugar. Simmer for 5 minutes.

Step 5

Add dissolved conrstarch and stir well. Simmer until sauce is thick.

Step 6

Toss fried pork in the sauce until well coated. Serve immediately.
